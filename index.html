Guilt - Final Degree Project

Puzzle and exploration game focused around an emergent narrative told through level and gameplay design. This game was made as a Final Degree Work for my degree in Video Game Design and Development at University Jaume I, which had the aim of exploring emergent narrative as a rare but potent form of storytelling in videogames. I partook in this project as a solo developer, focusing my efforts in the design aspect of it.

Read More Itch.io Watch Trailer

The game makes use of environmental design and various resources (such as visual flows using lighting) to guide the player without the need of a external HUD.

In this screenshot for example, the central position of the gramophone and the outside lighting being focused on it, guide the player into approaching and interacting with it the moment they enter the room.

One of the themes touched upon is the passage of time, which design-wise is represented by a game mechanic that allows the player to interact with certain objects that transport them between two different eras.

Being able to interact with the environment in both of these eras further repercusses the game. Where, for example, opening certain rooms in the past era gives access to them in the current one.

Narrative-wise, the story isn't directly told the player, instead relying fully on how they interpret the environment and the interactable objects within it.

Said story has been designed so that no canonical interpration exists, allowing each player to intepret their own based on their own experiences.

The game also counts with several, more interactable puzzles, that reward exploration. These provide the player with further context in order to help them expand their story.

Projekt: Privacy Invasion

Educational investigation game inspired by the gameplay of Papers, Please! that seeks to teach young audiencies about the many scams and dangers of the Internet. It was developed in Unity by a team of 3, where I was in charge of most of the programming, and part of the UI/UX design and navigation. I also took part in writing the narrative of some of the clients who the player interacts with inside the game.

Read More Itch.io View Gameplay

The player works as an investigator whose job is taking requests from clients that wish to know if they are being scammed or attacked through different apps.

As the player progresses, the money and recognition resources will change based on the choices made by the player (such as playing with honesty or accepting a bribe to let a scam through), affecting which ending will be achieved.

The narrative of some of the clients also progresses, unveileing their own little stories and providing more gameplay options for the player.

After the player accepts a request, they have to open the respective app mentioned by the client to analyze the possible scamming that is being performed. Each app has somewhat different interfaces that mirror that of the real life apps that they were inspired by.

After a case has been analyzed, the player can interact again with the client and select among the options that they deem best fit the case. Giving the specific reasoning, in the case that it's a scam, of why.

To help better indentify which cases are actual scams and why, the player counts with a notebook that explains the different kinds of scams that exist and signs to recognize them.

Aqua

Puzzle adventure game developed for the Spanish water company FACSA. Its focus is in combating the abandonment of the many subterranean water sources in the city of Castellón and its replacement for the more contamining plastic bottling.

Read More Itch.io View Gameplay

The navigation consists in a point & click adventure where the player traverses the map, looking for other characters that are in need of help and in turn will help the player progress.

Interacting with the different characters makes use of the implemented dialogue system, that makes use of software that simulates the speech sounds of Animal Crossing to voice each of the characters.

Each of the interactible characters gives the player a different mission that is completed by solving a puzzle. These puzzles entail different types of gameplay that evolve throughout the game, increasing the difficulty as the player gets accustomed to them.
